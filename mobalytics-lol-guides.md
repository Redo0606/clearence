### 2. Wave Management Guide

**Category:** Guides | **Author:** Mobalytics | **Updated:** Dec 15, 2025

**Source:** [mobalytics.gg/lol/guides/wave-management](https://mobalytics.gg/lol/guides/wave-management)

Wave Management is one of the most beneficial subjects to learn in League of Legends thanks to it being required in every single lane. Everybody has heard of wave management, but not everybody knows how to use it the right way.

**Key Concepts:**
- Minions spawn at 1:05 and continue every 30 seconds
- Standard wave: 6 minions (3 melee, 3 caster)
- Cannon wave: 7 minions (3 melee, 1 siege, 3 caster)
- Every third wave is a cannon wave until 20 minutes

**Minion Priority System:**
1. The closest enemy champion
2. The closest enemy minion
3. Enemy champions attacking an allied minion
4. Enemy turrets attacking an allied minion
5. Enemy minions attacking an allied minion
6. Enemy minions attacking an allied champion
7. Enemy champions attacking an allied champion

### 3. How to Ward in League of Legends

**Category:** Guides | **Author:** Mobalytics | **Updated:** Jan 13, 2026

**Source:** [mobalytics.gg/lol/guides/warding-guide](https://mobalytics.gg/lol/guides/warding-guide)

Warding in League of Legends is incredibly important for every role and every game you play. It is a necessity for everyone to know when to ward, where to ward and how to become better at warding.

**Ward Types:**
- **Warding Totem (Yellow/Green):** Invisible, 90s base duration, 3 HP, max 3 on map
- **Control Ward:** Visible, no time limit, reveals/disables enemy wards, 4 HP
- **Oracle Lens (Red):** 10s activation, reveals wards and stealth traps
- **Farsight Alteration (Blue):** Available at level 9, 4000 unit range, 2s vision

**Key Tips:**
- Never have 2 stacks on your Trinket in early game
- Supports should switch to Oracle Lens after completing warding quest
- Junglers should pick up Oracle Lens for ganking
- Control Wards in high-traffic areas for objective control

### 8. Everything You Need to Know About Roaming in LoL

**Category:** Guides | **Author:** Mobalytics | **Updated:** Dec 15, 2025

**Source:** [mobalytics.gg/lol/guides/everything-you-need-to-know-about-roaming-in-lol](https://mobalytics.gg/lol/guides/everything-you-need-to-know-about-roaming-in-lol)

Roaming in League of Legends is one of the most overlooked strategies. If done correctly, roaming can apply pressure in multiple lanes at once.

**What is Roaming?** Moving from one lane to another to gank, help allies, get kills, take objectives, or ward.

**Key Principles:**
- Roaming is usually done early to mid game
- Requires good setup (wave state) to avoid falling behind
- Every champion can roam, but some thrive (e.g., Alistar vs Janna)
- Difference from "rotating": Roaming = ganking; Rotating = moving for objectives
